About Me

– ◻ ×

I'm Tushar Borse, from Mumbai, India, with a strong interest in geospatial technology and how it's used to understand and shape the real world. My foundation started early, growing up around maps, instruments, and field discussions my father is a surveyor, and watching him work with surveying and GIS equipment gave me my first exposure to spatial thinking long before I knew the term "GIS."

Seeing how geographic data translated directly into roads, land boundaries, and planning decisions made a lasting impression on me. It showed me that GIS isn't just software or maps on a screen it's a decision-making tool that connects data to real outcomes. That early exposure naturally pushed me toward technology, systems, and eventually geospatial work.

Over time, my focus has grown beyond just tools to understanding how GIS solutions are designed, communicated, and adopted. I'm particularly interested in how complex technical ideas are presented clearly, how systems are evaluated before being implemented, and how good technical decisions can create long-term value. I'm obsessed with learning how geospatial technology can be used not just correctly, but effectively in a way that people trust and rely on.

My goal is to keep building depth in geospatial systems while developing the ability to explain, demonstrate, and shape solutions that make sense in real-world contexts. This site is a place where I explore ideas, document what I learn, and refine how I think about GIS, technology, and impact.
